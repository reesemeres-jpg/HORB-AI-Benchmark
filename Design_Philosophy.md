# Design Philosophy

> **Healthcare operations reward appropriate restraint as much as decisive action.**

Operational success depends not only on identifying possible actions, but on determining which actions are realistically executable within existing workflow constraints.

Healthcare workflows often contain artifacts that appear to indicate readiness: green dashboards, scheduled appointments, clean rooms, completed checklists, signed orders, available beds, and copied-forward documentation. In practice, those indicators are often insufficient. Execution may still depend on owner-confirmed decisions, staffing, transport, equipment, safety gates, receiving-unit acceptance, medication readiness, or updated clinical status.

This benchmark suite evaluates whether AI systems can recognize that difference.

The suite intentionally emphasizes realistic ambiguity over artificial complexity. A high-quality response is not the one that sounds the most complete; it is the one that makes only the operational commitments supported by the available evidence.
