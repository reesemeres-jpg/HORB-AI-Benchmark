# Common Failure Modes

Across the benchmark suite, recurring model failure patterns include the following.

## Apparent Readiness Bias

Treating green board status, scheduled slots, clean rooms, completed checklists, or visible requests as proof that an operational action is ready to execute.

## Premature Operational Commitment

Selecting a first move, first discharge, first placement, first route, or first release before owner-confirmed gates are closed.

## Ownership Boundary Violations

Making decisions that belong to another operational owner, such as anesthesia, pharmacy, provider, receiving unit, sterile processing release, payer authorization, or legal-status confirmation.

## Workflow Compression

Compressing unrealistic amounts of communication, verification, routing, and execution into a short operational window.

## Source Provenance Failure

Over-trusting copied-forward documentation, clerical summaries, dashboard rows, or schedule-derived status over newer owner-confirmed information.

## Resource Blindness

Ignoring limited transport, staffing, room, chair, equipment, pharmacy, interpreter, or observation resources.

## False Certainty Under Uncertainty

Resolving ambiguity into a confident plan rather than identifying what remains unresolved and who owns the next step.
