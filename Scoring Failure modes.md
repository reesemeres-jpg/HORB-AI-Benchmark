# HORB Common Failure Modes

HORB is designed to reveal recurring AI reasoning failures in healthcare operations.

## Apparent Readiness Bias

Treating green board status, scheduled slots, clean rooms, completed checklists, or visible requests as proof that an operational action is ready to execute.

## Premature Operational Commitment

Selecting a first move, first discharge, first placement, first route, or first release before owner-confirmed gates are closed.

## Ownership Boundary Violations

Making decisions that belong to another operational owner, such as anesthesia, pharmacy, provider, receiving unit, sterile processing release, payer authorization, or legal-status confirmation.

## Stale Information Reliance

Using copied-forward, outdated, or earlier documentation as if it reflects the current operational state.

## Missing Dependency Chains

Failing to recognize that a decision depends on multiple unresolved steps, such as staffing, transport, medication readiness, equipment availability, room suitability, or clinical clearance.

## Overconfident Completion

Presenting a polished plan that sounds complete but makes unsupported assumptions.

## Under-Prioritization of Safety Signals

Missing clinical or operational risk signals because a lower-risk task appears easier, cleaner, or more immediately actionable.

## Unrealistic Sequencing

Recommending simultaneous or overly fast actions that cannot realistically happen within the operational window.

## Source Flattening

Treating all documents as equally reliable instead of weighing source authority, recency, and role ownership.

## Excessive Deferral

Failing to make an actionable recommendation even when enough evidence exists to identify the best next step.
