# Project Summary

## Healthcare Operational Reasoning Benchmark Suite v1.0

**Author:** Meredith Reese, RN

This portfolio project contains 12 original healthcare operational reasoning benchmarks. Each benchmark includes a fictional use case, system instructions, user prompt, source documents, and strict grading guidelines.

The suite evaluates whether AI systems can distinguish apparent operational readiness from actual executable readiness while respecting ownership boundaries, temporal constraints, workflow dependencies, and resource limitations.

## Suite Contents

- **HORB-001 — Labor & Delivery Charge Nurse / OB Triage Flow** (Labor & Delivery Operations)
- **HORB-002 — Sterile Processing / OR Case-Cart Readiness Flow** (Sterile Processing and Perioperative Operations)
- **HORB-003 — Home Health Visit Coordination / Field Nursing Route** (Home Health Operations)
- **HORB-004 — Outpatient Infusion Center / Oncology Treatment Readiness** (Outpatient Infusion and Oncology Operations)
- **HORB-005 — Hospice On-Call / Symptom Crisis & Visit Routing** (Hospice Operations)
- **HORB-006 — Emergency Department Triage / Room Assignment & Ambulance Offload Flow** (Emergency Department Operations)
- **HORB-007 — Perioperative OR Add-On / Turnover Flow** (Perioperative Operations)
- **HORB-008 — Behavioral Health Crisis Unit / Psychiatric Intake & Safety Placement** (Behavioral Health Operations)
- **HORB-009 — Hospital Pharmacy / Medication Reconciliation & Discharge Readiness** (Hospital Pharmacy and Transitions of Care)
- **HORB-010 — Hospital Case Management / Discharge Disposition & Throughput** (Case Management and Discharge Planning)
- **HORB-011 — Perioperative Pre-Admission Testing / Day-of-Surgery Clearance** (Perioperative Pre-Admission Testing)
- **HORB-012 — Primary Care Same-Day Triage / Schedule Routing Flow** (Primary Care Operations)


## Core Methodology

The suite simulates short operational decision windows across healthcare settings. Each benchmark contains multiple plausible operational actions, conflicting or evolving documentation, and realistic resource constraints. Models are evaluated on judgment, prioritization, and executable planning rather than factual recall alone.
