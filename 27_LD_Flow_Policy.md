# L&D Flow Policy

Labor and Delivery Flow Workflow

1. L&D board color is a starting point, not final transfer, admission, induction, or OR readiness.
2. Labor-room movement requires current fetal/maternal status, provider acceptance, nurse assignment, and monitoring plan.
3. Postpartum transfer requires stable bleeding/fundal assessment, completed handoff, receiving-unit acceptance, and transport capacity.
4. Induction start requires room, nurse, medication/GBS plan, fetal monitoring, and provider order readiness.
5. Triage priority should account for fetal movement complaints, prior uterine surgery with pain, rupture status, active labor, and maternal warning signs.
6. OB OR readiness requires anesthesia, neonatal, equipment, blood bank/type-screen considerations, and staffing.
7. Transport should be staged only after destination and receiving side are settled.

Operational reminder: Creating space by moving a patient before clinical/receiving gates close can create a bounce-back or delay a higher-risk arrival.
