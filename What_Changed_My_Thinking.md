# What Changed My Thinking

Initially, I assumed that increasing document complexity would create stronger evaluations.

Instead, I found that simplifying task instructions while increasing operational realism often produced richer reasoning behavior.

Scenarios with a single clearly correct operational choice frequently measured information retrieval more than operational judgment. The strongest evaluations emerged when multiple operationally plausible actions existed simultaneously, requiring prioritization rather than simple identification of the correct answer.

This shifted the project from testing whether a model could find the right fact to testing whether it could make the best operational decision under uncertainty.
