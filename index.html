Healthcare Operational Reasoning Benchmark Suite

An original benchmark suite for evaluating AI operational reasoning across realistic healthcare workflows.

Central Evaluation Question

Can an AI distinguish apparent operational readiness from actual executable readiness?

About the Project

This project contains 12 original healthcare operational reasoning benchmarks. Each benchmark uses fictional source documents, system instructions, prompts, and grading guidelines to evaluate operational judgment under uncertainty.

Benchmark Suite

HORB-001

Labor & Delivery Charge Nurse / OB Triage Flow

Labor & Delivery Operations

Evaluates whether an AI system can coordinate a Labor & Delivery flow window involving OB triage arrivals, labor-room admissions, postpartum transfers, scheduled inductions, possible urgent C-section evaluation, fetal monitoring changes, anesthesia coverage, Mother-Baby capacity, transport limits, medication readiness, and OR readiness.

Operational reasoning under uncertainty in a multidisciplinary obstetric workflow

HORB-002

Sterile Processing / OR Case-Cart Readiness Flow

Sterile Processing and Perioperative Operations

Evaluates whether an AI system can reason through case-cart release decisions involving sterilizer cycle status, biological indicators, loaner/vendor documentation, preference-card changes, instrument counts, scope reprocessing, missing instruments, partial carts, runner availability, and OR communication.

Operational readiness reasoning for sterile processing and OR case-cart release

HORB-003

Home Health Visit Coordination / Field Nursing Route

Home Health Operations

Evaluates whether an AI system can coordinate home health visit routing while balancing start-of-care readiness, wound VAC supply and order issues, urgent medication-safety messages, field nurse and LPN scope, access problems, caregiver availability, DME delivery, time-sensitive labs, route geography, and limited office-call capacity.

Field-route operational reasoning under uncertainty

HORB-004

Outpatient Infusion Center / Oncology Treatment Readiness

Outpatient Infusion and Oncology Operations

Evaluates whether an AI system can coordinate an outpatient infusion readiness window involving chemotherapy and biologic treatment readiness, lab parameters, pharmacy compounding, prior authorization, blood product compatibility, port access, chair capacity, nurse assignments, and patient communication.

Treatment-readiness reasoning across pharmacy, nursing, authorization, and chair capacity constraints

HORB-005

Hospice On-Call / Symptom Crisis & Visit Routing

Hospice Operations

Evaluates whether an AI system can coordinate hospice on-call routing while balancing symptom crisis calls, comfort-kit readiness, medication order ownership, DME/oxygen status, caregiver availability, nurse visit capacity, after-hours escalation, and realistic visit routing.

Hospice visit-routing and symptom-crisis operational reasoning

HORB-006

Emergency Department Triage / Room Assignment & Ambulance Offload Flow

Emergency Department Operations

Evaluates whether an AI system can coordinate ED triage and room assignment while balancing patient acuity, room suitability, ambulance offload, nurse/tech coverage, safety risks, isolation, monitoring needs, and evolving waiting-room information.

Emergency Department operational prioritization and room-assignment reasoning

HORB-007

Perioperative OR Add-On / Turnover Flow

Perioperative Operations

Evaluates whether an AI system can coordinate perioperative add-on and turnover flow involving OR room status, case urgency, anesthesia coverage, PACU capacity, instrumentation, patient readiness, vendor/equipment needs, transport, and short-window huddle preparation.

OR add-on sequencing and turnover operational reasoning

HORB-008

Behavioral Health Crisis Unit / Psychiatric Intake & Safety Placement

Behavioral Health Operations

Evaluates whether an AI system can coordinate behavioral health intake and placement while balancing legal status, medical clearance, contraband/security search, observation level, sitter coverage, room safety, unit acceptance, transportation, and evolving patient behavior.

Psychiatric intake placement reasoning with safety, legal, and staffing constraints

HORB-009

Hospital Pharmacy and Transitions of Care

Evaluates whether an AI system can coordinate transitions-of-care medication readiness while balancing discharge orders, pharmacist verification, renal dosing, duplicate therapies, anticoagulation, insulin, counseling, interpreter needs, prior authorization, meds-to-beds, and discharge timing.

Medication reconciliation and discharge-readiness operational reasoning

HORB-010

Hospital Case Management / Discharge Disposition & Throughput

Case Management and Discharge Planning

Evaluates whether an AI system can coordinate case management discharge planning while balancing discharge orders, SNF or facility acceptance, payer authorization, PASRR/level-of-care requirements, DME, home oxygen, dialysis, wound VAC, caregiver readiness, transport, discharge lounge capacity, and family communication.

Discharge disposition and throughput operational reasoning

HORB-011

Perioperative Pre-Admission Testing / Day-of-Surgery Clearance

Perioperative Pre-Admission Testing

Evaluates whether an AI system can coordinate pre-admission testing and next-day surgical readiness while balancing anesthesia clearance, anticoagulant holds, GLP-1/NPO updates, respiratory symptoms, abnormal labs, cardiac clearance, interpreter/consent needs, OSA/CPAP recovery planning, vendor/implant readiness, callbacks, and OR schedule preservation.

Surgical readiness and cancellation-prevention operational reasoning

HORB-012

Primary Care Same-Day Triage / Schedule Routing Flow

Primary Care Operations

Evaluates whether an AI system can coordinate primary care same-day triage and routing while balancing appointment requests, nurse-visit slots, telehealth holds, parking-lot symptoms, portal updates, voicemail symptoms, pediatric calls, allergic-reaction screening, interpreter needs, provider slot capacity, front desk routing, and emergency escalation decisions.

Ambulatory triage and schedule-routing operational reasoning

Design Philosophy

Healthcare operations reward appropriate restraint as much as decisive action. This suite evaluates executable reasoning rather than confident completion.
