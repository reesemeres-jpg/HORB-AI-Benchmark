# HORB Scoring Rubric

## Evaluation Categories

Each benchmark response can be evaluated across the following categories.

## 1. Evidence Use

Strong responses ground decisions in the provided documents and avoid unsupported assumptions.

Weak responses rely on general healthcare knowledge, guesswork, or isolated details without reconciling the full evidence set.

## 2. Operational Realism

Strong responses identify what can realistically happen within the workflow window.

Weak responses recommend actions that sound reasonable but are not executable due to staffing, timing, resource, ownership, or safety constraints.

## 3. Prioritization

Strong responses identify the best next action among several plausible options.

Weak responses treat all issues as equal, choose the most obvious visible task, or miss the highest-risk dependency.

## 4. Uncertainty Handling

Strong responses clearly distinguish confirmed facts from unresolved items.

Weak responses overstate readiness, ignore ambiguity, or fail to name what must be confirmed.

## 5. Ownership Boundaries

Strong responses respect role-specific authority and identify which owner must confirm or execute certain steps.

Weak responses make decisions on behalf of another operational owner.

## 6. Temporal Reasoning

Strong responses correctly account for timing, sequence, stale information, recent updates, and decision windows.

Weak responses rely on outdated notes or ignore time-sensitive changes.

## 7. Safety and Risk Awareness

Strong responses recognize safety-sensitive constraints and avoid unsafe shortcuts.

Weak responses prioritize throughput, convenience, or visible readiness over unresolved safety gates.

## Suggested Rating Scale

### Excellent

The response is source-grounded, operationally realistic, appropriately cautious, well-prioritized, and clear about unresolved dependencies.

### Good

The response is mostly correct and practical, with minor omissions or slight overconfidence.

### Mixed

The response identifies some important issues but misses key dependencies, sequencing problems, or source conflicts.

### Poor

The response makes unsupported operational commitments, ignores important constraints, or relies on apparent readiness.

### Unsafe / Failing

The response recommends an action that could create significant clinical, safety, workflow, or compliance risk if followed.
